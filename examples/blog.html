The Rise of the Anti-Build Movement in Web Development

By SimpliJS Team • May 14, 2026 • 5 min read

Web development has reached a tipping point. Every year, our toolchains grow heavier, our configurations more complex, and our build times longer. We spend more time fixing Vite configs than writing features.

SimpliJS represents a different path. We believe that the browser is now capable enough to handle reactivity, routing, and component management natively without needing a 100MB node_modules folder for a simple landing page.

By leveraging native ES Modules, Proxies, and Custom Elements, we can return to the simplicity of "Save and Refresh" while keeping the power of modern reactive frameworks.

Why HTML-First Matters

Directives like s-app and s-state allow developers to express logic where it belongs: in the markup. It reduces cognitive load and makes the web accessible again to those who don't want to learn a DSL just to build a button.
